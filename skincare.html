Skincare

Your Skin Is Your Largest Organ!

Great skin comes from within and starts with your health and wellness. With proper nutrition and a healthy lifestyle, your skin will be healthier and more even-toned, with less blemishes and less signs of aging. Some damage can be reversed or slowed down with a routine that incorporates regular facials, the right active ingredients, and healthy skin care practices, as recommended by an esthetician or skin therapist knowledgeable in ingredients and your skin.

Esthetics is the application of various treatments to the skin to maintain its health and vitality. Estheticians are trained in skin wellness, helping their clients balance oil and moisture content and achieve a healthy, youthful complexion. All great skin care programs include the right balance of antioxidants, exfoliants, and sun protection. Bring your skincare products in when you come for your facial or skin analysis appointment and leave with a custom program that meets your specific needs for your skin, budget, and lifestyle.

Skin Treatments

Facials - Starting From $65.00

A facial is a good way for your therapist to get an understanding of your skin prior to suggesting more aggressive treatments. A favial generally includes makeup removal and skin cleansing, exfoliation by mechanical, enzymatic, or chemical means, steaming, extractions, facial massage, a treatment mask, serum/moisturizer, and sunblock. For most people, facials can be scheduled every four weeks, although your therapist may recommend a different schedule based on your individual needs. Services range from Back Facial, Acne Facial, Sinus Facial, Signature Facial, and Custom Anti-Aging Facial.

Chemical Peels - Starting From $75.00

A chemical peel is an exfoliation process very effective in treating a large range of skin concerns such as aging, sun damage, acne, mild scarring, improving overall skin brightness, and evening skin tone. Peels can be light, moderate, or deep. Light peels require no down time rom work or normal activities. Moderate peels may require a day or two of down time, and deep peels can require a week or more to allow the skin to fully heal. Estheticians who are not working in a medical setting perform light to moerate peels only.
